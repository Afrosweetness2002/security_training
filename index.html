VU1 Field Handbook

Plain-English study companion to Väktargrundutbildning del 1 — law, fire, first aid and the craft of guarding. 9 days · ~88 lesson hours · written + practical exam · follows FAP 573-1.

The one idea behind everything — A väktare has no police powers. Your authority is essentially the rights any citizen (envar) already has, exercised with training, inside your assignment, under FAP 573-1. Almost every exam answer traces back to this.

The road to a full licence

VU1 · 9 days · exam→ PYT · 160 h supervised→ VU2 · 5 days · exam→ Licence · work independently

01 · Rules of the industry

Regler som styr bevakningsverksamheten ~8 h

Who may run guarding services, who approves guards, and the legal frame it all sits in.

TermMeaning

Who controls the profession

  • Polismyndigheten — writes the rulebook → FAP 573-1
  • Länsstyrelsen — authorises companies + approves each guard + supervises
  • Base law above it all: Lag (1974:191) om bevakningsföretag

Remember — Company → needs authorisation. Person → needs approval. Rules come from the police; both permissions come from Länsstyrelsen. A guard must be identifiable and not confusable with police.

The legal hierarchy & the four grundlagar

Top = highest authority. A lower rule can never override a higher one — grundlag beats law beats regulation. This is why guard powers are so narrow.

The four grundlagar in detail

GrundlagWhat it is
Regeringsformen (RF)The main constitution. Its Ch. 2 holds the fundamental rights (protection against deprivation of liberty, searches, coercion) — the ceiling your envarsgripande and self-defence powers fit beneath
Tryckfrihetsförordningen (TF)Freedom of the Press Act + public access to official documents (offentlighetsprincipen)
Yttrandefrihetsgrundlagen (YGL)Comparable protection for other media — radio, TV, film, online
Successionsordningen (SO)Succession to the Swedish throne (the oldest, 1810; rarely relevant to guarding)

For the exam — Regeringsformen is the one that matters for guarding; its Ch. 2 rights are why your authority is limited. The test itself examines the ordinary laws below it (Brottsbalken, Rättegångsbalken).

Fundamental freedoms — RF 2 kap

The six "opinion freedoms" (opinionsfriheter) — Regeringsformen, Chapter 2, § 1

Every citizen is guaranteed these against the state (gentemot det allmänna):

FrihetFreedom
YttrandefrihetFreedom of expression
InformationsfrihetFreedom to obtain & receive information
MötesfrihetFreedom of assembly — to hold and attend meetings
DemonstrationsfrihetFreedom to demonstrate
FöreningsfrihetFreedom of association
ReligionsfrihetFreedom of religion

Why a guard learns these — They're held against the state, and a väktare is a private actor, not "det allmänna" — so they don't bind you the way they bind the police. You learn them to know what not to unlawfully interfere with. Blocking a lawful assembly, moving along people handing out flyers, or stopping someone praying steps on protected freedoms — and ties straight to the olaga frihetsberövande risk if you overreach.

Public space vs private property — the practical line

  • In genuinely public space, these freedoms are strong and can only be limited by law — e.g. a demonstration needs a police permit under ordningslagen, and assembly can be restricted for public order.
  • On a client's private property, the owner's rights let you (acting for them) set conditions and ask people to leave — the freedoms don't grant a right to demonstrate inside a private mall or to stay on private premises.

Links to religionsfrihet ↔ §08 discrimination (you can't treat someone worse for their religion), and the whole set ↔ §07 yrkesetik / bemötande (respecting people's rights is part of professional conduct).

02a · Security law

Bevakningsjuridik — the powers themselves ~16 h · biggest exam weight

The heart of the written exam. Everything here is a "freedom from liability" ground — an act that would normally be a crime but isn't, when done in a genuine defensive or emergency situation.

Self-defence — Nödvärn BrB 24:1

Repelling a started or imminent criminal attack on a person or property. The act is a crime only if it is clearly indefensible (uppenbart oförsvarlig) — that word "clearly" gives you real margin. You may also defend someone else.

The four situations: (1) started/imminent criminal attack; (2) resisting property taken back on the spot; (3) unlawful intrusion into room/house/yard/vessel; (4) refusing to leave a home after being told to.

Nödvärn (self-defence)Nöd (necessity) BrB 24:4
You act againstA person's criminal attackA danger (fire, accident, illness)
Loses protection whenClearly indefensible (uppenbart oförsvarlig)Merely indefensible (oförsvarlig) — narrower
ExampleRestraining someone attacking youBreak a window to save someone inside

Nödvärnsexcess BrB 24:6 — Even if you used too much force, you're excused if you "could hardly collect yourself" (svårligen besinna sig) in the moment.

Citizen's arrest — Envarsgripande RB 24:7

Exam trap — This lives in the Code of Judicial Procedure (Rättegångsbalken), not the Criminal Code.

Get it wrong and your "arrest" can itself be a crime — unlawful deprivation of liberty (olaga frihetsberövande, BrB Ch. 4).

Also: a guard on duty has extra protection — violence or threats against them can be våld/hot mot tjänsteman BrB Ch. 17.

Law reflex for any scenario — (1) name the rule + where it lives; (2) does the situation qualify? (3) did the response stay within limits (not clearly indefensible / necessary & proportionate / handed to police promptly)?

02b · Principles of intervention

De fem principerna, tvång och ändamål — the discipline that governs every power in §02a

§02a gave you the powers. This half gives you the test each one has to pass before, during and after you use it. In the written exam the powers earn you the marks and these principles decide whether you keep them.

The five principles

De fem principerna — the test every intervention has to pass

Five questions, in order. Each one is a gate: fail a single gate and the action is unlawful, no matter how well you do on the others.

PrincipThe questionYou fail it when
LegalitetsprincipenMay I? Is there a law that actually gives me this power?You act on "company policy", habit, or what the client wants. No legal basis = no action. RF 1:1 · BrB 1:1
ÄndamålsprincipenWhy am I doing it? Am I using the power for the purpose the law intended?You detain someone to teach them a lesson, or search a bag to satisfy curiosity. Right power, wrong reason — still unlawful
BehovsprincipenMust I? Is action needed at all, and is this the mildest measure that works?You go hands-on when talking would have worked — or you keep holding someone after the need has passed. Stop when the need stops
ProportionalitetsprincipenIs it worth it? Is the harm I cause in reasonable proportion to what I achieve?You take someone to the floor over a chocolate bar. The damage outweighs the gain
ObjektivitetsprincipenAm I being fair? Saklighet och opartiskhet — facts, not assumptions; everyone treated alikeYou decide who the thief is by how they look, then collect only the facts that fit. RF 1:9

Say it as five short questions — May I? · Why? · Must I? · Is it worth it? · Am I fair? Useful as a checklist — but the last one isn’t really last. Am I fair? has to be true before you start, not confirmed at the end.

One case through all five

Someone walks out of a shop with unpaid goods:

  • Legalitet — prison is in the range for theft and they're caught in the act → RB 24:7 gives me the power ✅
  • Ändamål — I'm holding them to hand them to police; not to punish, not to search them, not to extract a confession ✅
  • Behov — they're calm and cooperative, so a hand on the arm and a request to wait is enough. No cuffs ✅
  • Proportionalitet — goods worth 60 kr do not justify a takedown or a broken wrist ✅
  • Objektivitet — I write what I saw, including the parts that don't fit my theory ✅

The nuance the exam likes — Strictly, these principles bind public authorities (RF 1:9, and polislagen 8 § for the police), and a väktare is a private actor, not "det allmänna". But they are the standard you're trained, supervised and judged by — and behov and proportionalitet are already built into laga befogenhet BrB 24:2, the rule that actually governs your use of force. So they bite in practice even though they reach you indirectly.

Does the order matter?

Mostly yes — but not for all five, and the exception is worth understanding.

The tempting picture is a queue: check legality, then purpose, then need, then proportionality, then finally ask whether you were fair. A traffic stop seems to prove it — the police have the legal right to ask for your licence immediately, and can worry about objectivity later.

Why that picture is wrong — The objectivity didn’t come later. It came first. Before the car was ever pulled over, an officer had to judge objectively whether there was a reason to stop it — measuring the speed, watching the driving, deciding this car rather than that one. That decision is objektivitetsprincipen at work. It then continues through the whole stop and into the paperwork afterwards.

So the honest model is two-layered:

  • Legalitet and ändamål are genuine threshold questions — answered before you act, and if either fails you never start.
  • Behov and proportionalitet are continuous — re-asked every second you are acting. The need can end mid-grip, and when it does you must stop.
  • Objektivitet is not a step at all. It is a condition that has to hold before, during and after: when you decide who to approach, what you notice while you act, and what you put in the report.

The väktare’s angle — The numbered order in this handbook is written from your decision-making seat, not as a legal ranking. As a guard your very first move is almost always “do I have any power here at all?”, because usually the answer is no — which is why legalitet leads. A police officer starts further forward: their powers are broad and already established, so their live question is which of them to use, and on whom. Same five principles, different entry point. Objektivitet sits under both.

Legalitetsprincipen in depth — and the word tvång

Ingen makt utan lag — no power without law

Legalitetsprincipen is the first gate because it is the one that actually creates your authority. It has two halves worth separating:

  • For the citizen — no act is a crime, and no punishment may be imposed, unless a law said so before the act. BrB 1:1 · RF 2:10 (the ban on retroactive criminal law).
  • For anyone exercising power — den offentliga makten utövas under lagarna RF 1:1. Power flows from the law; it is never assumed, inherited from a uniform, or granted by a client.

Three things that are NOT a legal basis — (1) the client's wishes — a client cannot give you a power they don't have themselves; (2) the bevakningsinstruktion — it limits and directs your authority, it never creates it; (3) analogy — "this is close enough to a citizen's arrest" is not a power. If you cannot name the rule, you do not have the rule.

Tvång — coercion

Tvång means getting someone to do, tolerate or refrain from something against their will. That is the thing the law regulates — and the mistake guards make is assuming coercion only counts when hands are involved. It doesn't.

Direkt tvångIndirekt tvång
What it doesOverrides the body — the person is moved or held regardless of their willOverrides the will — the person "chooses", but only because you removed the real alternative
Looks likeGrip, restraint, carry-out, handcuffsThreat of a consequence, locked door, withheld ID, standing in the only exit
Feels like (to you)Obviously serious — you know you're using forceFeels harmless — which is exactly the trap
Where the power comes fromBrB 24:1 nödvärn · BrB 24:2 laga befogenhet · RB 24:7 envarsgripande · BrB 24:4 nödSame list. There is no separate, softer permission for "just words"
If you have no basisAssault (misshandel), unlawful deprivation of libertyUnlawful coercion (olaga tvång BrB 4:4), unlawful threat, unlawful deprivation of liberty

The test that catches both — Ask: "Is this person free to walk away right now?" If the honest answer is no — for any reason, whether your hand or your words put them there — you are using tvång, and you must be able to name the rule that permits it.

The everyday version — Asking someone to wait for the police is not coercion; they're free to refuse and leave. Telling them they may not leave — or standing in the doorway so they can't — is. The words are similar; the legal position is completely different. If you don't have RB 24:7, you may ask, and you must accept "no".

How the five principles land on tvång — Legalitet asks whether you may coerce at all. Ändamål asks whether you're coercing for the reason the rule exists. Behov pushes you down the ladder: voice before hands, and no coercion at all if a request works. Proportionalitet caps how hard. Objektivitet asks whether you'd coerce this person if they looked different.

Ändamålsprincipen in depth — a power may only serve its own purpose

Lagstöd får bara användas för sitt syfte

Every power in the law was created to solve one specific problem. Using it to solve a different problem — even a reasonable one, even one you care about — is a misuse, and the fact that you were entitled to the power in the first place does not save you.

The worked example — skyddsvisitation

A protective search (skyddsvisitation, the model for which is PL 19 §) exists for one reason: safety. You are looking for weapons and dangerous objects — things that could be used to harm you, the person, or a bystander.

The trap — A protective search is not an evidence search. If your hand is in someone’s pocket because you want the stolen goods rather than because you want to know whether there’s a knife in there, you have used a safety power to do police work. Right power, wrong purpose — still unlawful.

Ask yourself mid-action — “If I found nothing dangerous, would I stop searching?” If the honest answer is no — you’d keep going until you found the pen — then safety was never the real purpose, and the search is not what you’ll claim it was in the report.

Exam framing — Name the purpose out loud when you answer a scenario: “I searched for weapons and dangerous objects, for safety.” If the scenario tempts you into recovering property or gathering proof, that’s the trap being set. Defer to your instructor’s compendium for the exact scope of a väktare’s search rights — they are narrower than the police’s.

Case study — the cashier who was sure

Where objektivitet, legalitet and tvång all meet in one shift

A cashier tells you she watched a man take items. She kept an eye on him the whole time — while serving other customers, and while talking to you. She is certain.

First question: is she objective? — She may be completely honest and still be wrong. She was serving customers and holding a conversation; her attention was split. Believing her is not the same as knowing. Objektivitetsprincipen means you act on what you objectively know, not on how convincing the witness sounded.

What you may NOT do

ActionWhy not
Search himNo legal basis. A search needs a lawful detention behind it, and you don’t have one — you have someone else’s impression
Question himYou have no power to interrogate. A uniformed guard asking “did you take something?” puts real pressure on a person to answer — that pressure is indirekt tvång. Their freedom to say nothing is theirs to keep
Hold or block himSame problem, more visibly. Without RB 24:7 he is free to walk away at any moment

What you MAY do

  • Watch. Observation breaks no law and is most of the job.
  • Be visible. Say hello. Presence is lawful, often effective, and costs nobody a right.
  • Report what you were told — as what it is: a cashier’s account, not an established fact.

The twist — he leaves, the CCTV confirms it, and he comes back

You review the footage. He did take the items. Days later he walks back into the store. Can you seize him now?

No — and this surprises most students

The lecturer’s reason: you cannot be certain it is the same man. Recognising a face from footage is not identification. Only the police can positively identify a person; a väktare has no power and no reliable means to do it — and there is always a chance of seizing the wrong person, which is a serious wrong done to someone innocent.

The statutory reason, on top of it: RB 24:7 requires bar gärning eller flyende fot — caught in the act or in immediate flight. A man walking calmly back in days later is neither. The gate simply isn’t open.

What to do instead — The footage goes to the police, with a report. Identification and historic offences are their work, not yours. Your job was to observe accurately and record it honestly — and you did.

The through-line — Notice how little of this case is about force. Almost every wrong answer here is a quiet one: a question, a certainty borrowed from someone else, a hand on an arm because you recognised a face. A väktare has no police powers — and that limit bites hardest in exactly the moments when you feel most sure you’re right.

Security works in layers (defence in depth):

  1. Mekaniskt skydd — locks, grilles, safes, fencing → slow the intruder
  2. Detection — alarms, cameras → signal the larmcentral

06 · Fire

Brand ~10 h · practical

The fire triangle (brandtriangeln): fire needs heat (värme) + fuel (bränsle) + oxygen (syre) at once. Remove any one and it goes out — and each extinguishing method maps onto a side.

Fire classes (SS-EN3)

ClassWhat burnsUse
ASolids: wood, paper, textiles, most plasticsWater · foam · powder
BLiquids: petrol, oil, paint, solventsFoam · powder · CO₂ (no water)
CGases: LPG/gasol, natural gas, acetylenePowder
DMetals: magnesium, aluminiumSpecial metal agent only
FCooking fat & oil: fryers, kitchensSpecial kitchen extinguisher

Water only on Class A. Never on liquid, electrical or fat fires. CO₂ is the one for electrical (no residue, doesn't conduct). Old "Class E" for electrical is no longer used.

Smoke kills, not flame — evacuate first, stay low, keep an exit behind you. Drill: pull pin → aim at the base → squeeze → sweep.

07 · Professional ethics

Yrkesetik och moral ~8 h

You have a small amount of authority and a uniform that makes people assume you have far more. Ethics is the subject that governs what you do with that gap — and it quietly decides most scenario questions on the exam.

The four concepts

TermPlain English
EtikThe thinking. Systematic reasoning about what is right and wrong, and why. From Greek ethos — character, custom. Etik is the map
MoralThe doing. How you actually act when it costs you something. From Latin mores — customs. Moral is the route you actually walked
Socialt styrda reglerSocially governed rules — the unwritten expectations of a group: workplace culture, custom, courtesy, "how we do things here". Nobody voted on them and nobody wrote them down, but everyone notices when you break one
Hur man förhåller sig till etiska reglerHow you relate to ethical rules — knowing they exist, choosing consciously rather than by reflex or mood, and being able to justify the choice afterwards to a supervisor, a report, or a court

Etik vs moral — Both words originally meant "custom", which is why they get muddled. Keep them apart like this: etik is the reasoning, moral is the behaviour. Someone can hold sound etik and still act badly under pressure — that's a moral failure, not an intellectual one. Ethics training exists so the reasoning is already done before the pressure arrives.

Three kinds of rules

Every situation you meet on shift is governed by three separate rule systems at once. They usually agree. The interesting moments are when they don't.

Formell lagSocialt styrda reglerPersonlig moral
Comes fromParliament & authorities — BrB, RB, FAP 573-1, your bevakningsinstruktionThe group: colleagues, the client's site, society at largeYour own upbringing, conscience, values
Written down?YesRarely — you learn it by watchingNo
Enforced byCourts, employer, LänsstyrelsenSocial pressure: reputation, exclusion, complaintsYourself — guilt, self-respect
If you break itFine, prison, dismissal, lost godkännandeFrozen out, taken off the site, informal disciplineYou live with it
Guard exampleDetaining someone outside RB 24:7 — that's olaga frihetsberövandeDiscussing an incident loudly in the lunchroom; not relieving a colleague on timeLetting a hungry shoplifter go because it "felt right"

Where they pull apart — Law is the floor, not the ceiling. Something can be perfectly legal and still be wrong (technically-correct rudeness; filming someone because you can). And a socially governed rule can push you toward something unlawful — "everyone here does it", "the last guard always cuffed them". "Everyone does it" is never a defence. Your godkännande is personal, your liability is personal, and so is your report.

Three ways to judge an action

Same act, three different questions you can ask about it. Swedish course material calls these the three etiska modeller — all three turn up in scenario questions.

ModellJudges byExample
Sinnelagsetik (avsikt · varför · orsak)The intention behind itA starving child steals an apple to survive — we understand it, the motive is need. A billionaire steals the same apple: same act, but the motive isn't need, so we judge it differently
KonsekvensetikThe effect it producesDrivers slow down when a speed camera appears — behaviour follows consequences. Judge the act by what it actually causes, not by the rule it obeys
PliktetikDuty itselfYou do the right thing because it is the right thing — whether or not anyone is watching, and whatever it costs you

One scenario, three lenses

You catch someone shoplifting baby formula:

  • Avsikt — the motive looks like need, not greed. That should change your tone, your patience, how you speak to them
  • Konsekvens — what outcome actually helps here? A proportionate handover, not a spectacle in front of the shop
  • Plikt — RB 24:7 defines what you may do, and your bevakningsinstruktion defines what you must report. That doesn't bend because you feel sympathy

How a väktare holds all three — Pliktetik sets the floor: law and instruction are not negotiable. Intent and consequence then shape how you carry the duty out — your manner, your force, your discretion within the rules. Sympathy adjusts the manner, never the legality. An examiner marking a scenario wants both halves: correct legal action, humane delivery.

Working through a dilemma

Six questions, in this order

  1. Is it lawful? If no, stop — nothing below rescues an unlawful act.
  2. Is it inside my assignment? Bevakningsinstruktion, FAP 573-1 — authority you weren't given isn't yours to use.
  3. Whose interest does this serve? Client, public, or me? If the honest answer is "me", stop.
  4. Would it survive daylight? Could I describe this action in plain words to my supervisor — in a written report — in court?
  5. Would I do the same for anyone else? Swap the person's ethnicity, age, accent, clothing; if your answer changes, that's not judgement, it's bias.
  6. Act — then record it accurately. Facts, not opinions.

Ethics filter vs legal filter — The six questions above are the ethical filter. The five principles in §02b are the legal one. Run the legal five first — they decide whether you may act at all; ethics then decides how you act.

The everyday ethics of the job come down to three recurring duties: tystnadsplikt (what you learn on assignment stays there — §01), bemötande (how you treat people, including the ones you're removing), and opartiskhet (impartiality — no private grudges, no favours, no discounts for friends).

The exam's favourite instinct — Professionalism = lawful, impartial, respectful, discreet. When a scenario offers a "tough" response vs a "measured" one, the calm, rights-respecting answer is almost always correct. Never abuse the small authority you have.

Connects to §02b the five principles (the legal version of the same discipline) · §08 discrimination (the impartiality question in legal form) · §01 fundamental freedoms · §09 conflict management (de-escalation is applied ethics).

08 · Discrimination & behaviour

Olaga diskriminering och mänskliga beteenden ~4 h

  • Olaga diskriminering — a crime to treat someone unfavourably in service on a protected ground.
  • Protected grounds (diskrimineringsgrunder): sex, transgender identity/expression, ethnicity, religion/belief, disability, sexual orientation, age.

One line to carry — Judge behaviour and facts — never identity or appearance. Reading human behaviour under stress (fear, aggression, intoxication) is the bridge into conflict management.

09 · Conflict & self-protection

Konflikthantering och självskydd ~8 h · practical

Konflikttrappan — conflict escalates step by step, so aim every move at stepping down.

On the mat — Physical självskydd is the last resort, and only ever within nödvärn limits (defensible force, never retaliation). Follow the instructor's technique — it's not sparring or an MMA cage. Keep distance, don't get cornered, call for backup early.

10 · Handcuffs

Handfängsel ~4 h · practical

Three gates must all be present before you cuff someone:

Cuffing is coercion — never for convenience. Then a duty of care: monitor the person, don't leave them in a dangerous position, hand to police promptly. Wrongful cuffing can itself be assault or unlawful deprivation of liberty.

11 · Emergency first aid

Akutsjukvård / HLR ~6 h · practical · follows Svenska HLR-rådet

The numbers — 30 : 2 (compressions : breaths) · 100–120 per minute · 5–6 cm depth. Let the chest come fully back up each time; each breath ~1 s until the chest rises; minimise interruptions; continue until help takes over.

  • Children: start with 5 initial breaths, then continue (a layperson may use 30:2). If alone with no phone, ~1 min CPR before calling.
  • Breathing but unconscious → recovery position (stabilt sidoläge).
  • Severe bleeding → firm direct pressure, elevate, call 112.
  • Choking → encourage coughing → back blows → abdominal thrusts; if they collapse, start CPR.

This is a sensitive, real-world topic — a certified in-person HLR course (many run free) is the right place to build real confidence.

Quick self-test

Q1 · Which law contains envarsgripande?
Rättegångsbalken (Code of Judicial Procedure) 24:7, 2nd paragraph — not the Criminal Code.
Q2 · What must the offence meet for a citizen's arrest?
Prison must be in the penalty range, and the person caught in the act or fleeing.
Q3 · What do you do immediately after detaining someone?
Hand them to the nearest police officer promptly (skyndsamt).
Q4 · Someone spits at you — can you punch them back under self-defence?
No. That would be clearly indefensible (uppenbart oförsvarlig).
Q5 · Nödvärn vs nöd — which has the narrower margin?
Nöd (necessity): merely oförsvarlig loses protection, vs uppenbart oförsvarlig for self-defence.
Q6 · Name the five principles, in order.
Legalitet · ändamål · behov · proportionalitet · objektivitet — May I? Why? Must I? Is it worth it? Am I fair?
Q7 · Etik or moral — which is the reasoning, which is the behaviour?
Etik is the reasoning (the map); moral is the behaviour (the route you actually walked).
Q8 · Which ethical model judges an act by the intention behind it?
Sinnelagsetik (avsikt) — the starving child and the apple. Konsekvensetik judges by outcome; pliktetik by the rule.
Q9 · You block a doorway so someone can't leave, but never touch them. Is that coercion?
Yes — indirekt tvång. No physical contact is needed. If they aren't free to walk away, you're coercing, and you need a legal basis (RB 24:7) or it can be olaga tvång BrB 4:4 / unlawful deprivation of liberty.
Q10 · Can your client or your bevakningsinstruktion give you a power?

30:2, 100–120 per minute, 5–6 cm deep.
Q12 · Which fire class is water wrong for?
Everything except Class A — never on liquid (B), gas (C), metal (D), fat (F) or electrical.
Q13 · What crime might you commit by wrongly detaining someone?
Unlawful deprivation of liberty (olaga frihetsberövande, BrB Ch. 4).
